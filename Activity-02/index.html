History of Photography

"Photography, as we know it today, began in the early 1800s in France when inventor Joseph Nicéphore Niépce created a prototype of a photographic camera in 1816. He used a portable camera obscura to expose a pewter plate coated with bitumen to light. This is the first recorded image that did not fade quickly. He is credited for taking the oldest surviving photograph, dated from 1826, of a view from the window of his house."
