The universe has no centre and is constantly expanding (getting bigger) every second making it impossible to reach the edge.

The observable universe is estimated to be about 93 billion light-years in diameter. This vast expanse contains over 100 billion galaxies, each with millions or even billions of stars, showcasing the immense scale and complexity of the cosmos.
